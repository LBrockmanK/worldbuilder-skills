# Rubric: Writing-Doctrine Blind Trial

## How to score

Read all six notes blind, in packet-number order: `out/note-1.md` through
`out/note-6.md`. Do not open `key.md` yet.

Score each note's row in the scoring table as you finish reading it, with
one exception: leave the Preference rank column blank until all six notes
are read. Once you have read all six, rank them 1 through 6 against each
other, forced, no ties.

For the three 1-5 scales (Behavioral specificity, Stageability,
Liveliness): 1 means the note fails the criterion throughout the read; 5
means a full read turns up no violations.

Do not decode `key.md` until every cell in the table below is filled,
including the rank column. Decoding early biases the read.

Column definitions:

- **Behavioral specificity (1-5):** entries describe enactable behavior,
  not labels.
- **Stageability (1-5):** a director could stage the sentences.
- **Liveliness (1-5):** the present state contains live, competing pulls
  a scene could pick up.
- **Trait-word leakage (count):** heavy trait adjectives of the
  poisoning class (intelligent, analytical, arrogant, dominant, and
  kin).
- **Slop density (count):** hits against the slop-phrase checklist
  (`docs/slop-phrases.md` at the plugin root; a review reference for the
  scorer, applied by hand here, never shipped to the arms).
- **Coverage (n/4):** count of four required elements present:
  contradiction present; irrational behavior with root; self-image gap;
  relationship anchors met.
- **Length (words):** word count of the note body. Recorded, not scored.
- **Preference rank (1-6):** forced rank across all six notes, no ties.
