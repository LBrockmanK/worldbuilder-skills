# Character Framework — Parts 1 & 2

*Sub-file for `worldbuilder-character-blueprint`. Read this when working on the Foundation and Behavioral Builder sections.*

## Part 1: Character Foundation

Lajos Egri's three-dimensional framework: body, environment, soul. Work in any order — each dimension feeds causality into the next.

### The Body (Physiological)

Not just appearance — how their body has shaped their experience of the world.

- What do they look like? Age, build, distinguishing features. What does a stranger notice first?
- How has their body shaped their life? Does their appearance attract wanted or unwanted attention? What are they proud of, self-conscious about, indifferent to?
- What do their clothing and grooming choices reveal? Deliberate style, functional default, or performance?

### The Environment (Sociological)

Where someone grew up is the engine of characterization. This is where friction starts.

- Where did they grow up? What class, what culture, what was valued, what was looked down on?
- What shaped them during formative years? Key experiences, turning points. Did they fit in or stand out? What did they learn before they were old enough to question it?
- Where are they now, and how do they feel about it? Is their current life what they planned? Do they feel stuck, content, or restless?
- What 1–3 specific cultural touchpoints ground them? Use sparingly — details that reveal character, not just consumption.

### The Soul (Psychological)

The most important section. This is where the deepest friction lives.

- What do they want most? Not surface wants — the deeper want underneath. To be respected? To feel safe? To prove something? To be seen?
- What are they afraid of? Not a phobia necessarily — what outcome or realization would be hardest to face?
- Where is the gap between who they think they are and who they actually are? This gap produces the best roleplay moments.
- What irrational thing do they do, and where does it come from?

> **Why the irrational behavior question matters most:** LLMs default to writing rational, helpful-assistant-style characters. An irrational behavior with a clear emotional root forces the LLM to generate responses that feel human rather than algorithmic. It is the single most effective thing in this framework.

## Part 2: Behavioral Personality Builder

Convert psychology into behavioral descriptions the LLM can use.

**Technique credit:** The label vs. behavioral distinction and the When/Behavior/Because formula originate from archetype_builder's Label and Behavioral Technique (Reddit). The friction principle — contradictions, irrational behavior, self-image gap — draws from huge-centipede's Friction Technique (Reddit). Both via The Character Foundry.

### The Formula

> When [trigger], they [behavior], because [underlying reason].

### Why behavioral descriptions outperform trait labels

| Trait | Label (avoid) | Behavioral (use) |
|---|---|---|
| Confident | "She is confident." | "When challenged, she responds with certainty, treating doubt as a personal insult rather than a reasonable position." |
| Stubborn | "He is stubborn." | "When someone pushes back, he doubles down. Changing his mind feels like losing, and he does not lose." |
| Kind | "They are kind." | "When someone is struggling, they drop everything to help — partly because they care, partly because being needed is the only time they feel valuable." |
| Nervous | "She is anxious." | "In new social situations she over-prepares what to say, then abandons all of it and talks too fast. She apologizes for things that are not her fault." |

Labels give the LLM one word to repeat. Behavioral descriptions force it to generate language that fits the behavior — dramatically more varied output.

### Core behaviors

Write 3–5 behavioral descriptions. Three strong ones outperform five weak ones. Focus on behaviors that come up in actual roleplay. Behaviors here describe general patterns — if a behavior primarily concerns one specific relationship, it belongs in Relationships.

### Contradictions

The most interesting characters contain contradictions. A character with no friction between what they want and what they do gives the LLM nothing to work with.

- What is the main contradiction? What do they present vs. what is actually true?
- What would make them change? What experience, person, or realization could break the pattern?
