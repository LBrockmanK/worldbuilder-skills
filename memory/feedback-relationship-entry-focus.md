---
name: feedback-relationship-entry-focus
description: Relationship entries describe the character's OWN experience of the other person — not the other person's qualities, not the relationship from both sides
metadata:
  type: feedback
---

Each relationship entry in a character note describes how THIS character experiences the other person. It is not a summary of the other person's traits, and it is not a description of the relationship from both sides.

**Wrong:** "Isla Cord: Decades together with the quality of long love: completely familiar and still occasionally surprising. She talks; he remembers." — "She talks; he remembers" describes her behavior, not his experience of the relationship.

**Right:** "Isla Cord: Long years together, and she is the one person he cannot fully deflect. Her directness sometimes produces the honest answer from him before he has decided to give it." — This describes what the relationship IS to him and how it affects him.

**Why:** Relationship entries are reference material for how the AI should render this character's behavior and emotional responses toward specific people. That requires knowing what the relationship feels like FROM this character's side. A description of the other person belongs in their own note.

**How to apply:** Every relationship entry should answer: what does this person mean to this character? What does the character notice, feel, or do differently because of this person? Not: what is the other person like?
