---
name: feedback-positive-statements
description: Character descriptions say what things ARE, not what they AREN'T — avoid opening with or relying on negative constructions for characterization
metadata:
  type: feedback
---

Character notes should describe what a character is, does, thinks, and feels using positive statements. Negative constructions ("not the quiet one," "not unfriendly," "does not believe in magic") make the AI work harder and give it less to work with.

**Wrong:** "Not the quiet one. Where Emrys watches and waits, Isla engages." — Opens with a negative label before saying anything real.

**Wrong:** "He does not believe in magic as a category." — Says what he doesn't believe before saying what he does.

**Right:** "Isla talks. She has opinions she gives without being asked." — States what she is directly.

**Right:** "He has a framework for things that happen that he can't explain. That framework is built from observation." — States what he does believe.

**Why:** The AI generates character behavior from positive material. Negatives tell it what to avoid but not what to do. A character described as "not cold" gives the AI less than one described as "warm in the particular way of someone who has stopped monitoring their own warmth."

**Exceptions:** Negatives are fine in specific factual contexts ("she has not sent the letter," "he hasn't asked") where the un-done thing is the meaningful information. The rule is about characterization — labels, traits, personality — not factual states of affairs.
