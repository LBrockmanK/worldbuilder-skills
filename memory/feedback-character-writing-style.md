Outside of Example Dialogue sections, all writing should be clear, concise, and concrete. Prefer Anglo-Saxon words that name real things directly. Avoid Latinate vocabulary, which tends toward the abstract and poetic.

Examples of what to avoid: "rationalization," "temporal," "ephemeral," "liminal," "accumulated," "sustain that framing," "the one who stayed," "filed this immediately," "precise about who he lets see that." These sound like writing rather than meaning something.

Examples of what to prefer: "she knows," "he works," "she hasn't asked," "he keeps it to himself," "she doesn't trust it yet." Short words, active verbs, real things.

**Why:** User has corrected this pattern multiple times across relationship entries and other card sections. Flowery or Latinate phrasing obscures meaning and makes character notes harder to use as reference material. Only Example Dialogue earns stylistic voice.

**How to apply:** Every sentence outside of Example Dialogue should pass the test: does this say a specific thing plainly? If it reads like prose style rather than a clear fact or observation, rewrite it with simpler words and a direct claim. Applies to all sections of the character note: Concept, Foundation, Behavioral Descriptions, Relationships, Relationship Behavior, Appearance.
